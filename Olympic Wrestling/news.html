Induct John Smith Into the Olympic Hall of Fame

What more does John Smith need to do? Winning six consecutive World and Olympic titles from 1987 to 1992 set the standard for American wrestling. No U.S. wrestler had won more than two in a row before him. Only three Americans have won two Olympic wrestling golds: Smith, Bruce Baumgartner, and George Mehnert. Yet, only two wrestlers—Gable and Baumgartner—are in the U.S. Olympic Hall of Fame. Smith isn’t one of them. Unlike sports with multiple events per Games, wrestlers have one shot every four years. Smith seized his chances and dominated. Recognized as one of the 100 Greatest Olympians in 1996, he's in every major hall of fame—except this one. This oversight must end. Induct John Smith and pave the way for other greats like Kenny Monday, Rulon Gardner, and Jordan Burroughs. Wrestling deserves better.

Azerbaijan to Host 2026 World Judo Championships

The International Judo Federation has announced that Azerbaijan will host the 2026 World Judo Championships, marking the country's return as a host after the 2018 edition. This major event will kick off the Olympic qualification period for the Los Angeles 2028 Games. With strong support from President Ilham Aliyev, Azerbaijan has become a judo powerhouse, highlighted by Olympic gold medals from Hidayat Heydarov and Zelym Kotsoiev at Paris 2024. The nation also plays a key role in judo education, hosting an IJF Academy regional office in Baku. Set to take place in a country with deep sporting traditions and top-tier infrastructure, the 2026 World Judo Championships promise an unforgettable competition. The IJF and Azerbaijan Judo Federation look forward to welcoming the global judo community for this prestigious event.

Paris 2024 judo: All results, as Lasha Bekauri of Georgia takes home second straight Olympic gold medal in men’s -90kg

Lasha Bekauri of Georgia secured gold in the men’s -90kg judo event at Paris 2024, defeating Japan’s Murao Sanshiro 10-1 on July 31. It marks Bekauri’s second consecutive Olympic title after his victory at Tokyo 2020. Tied at 1-1, he sealed the win with an ippon in the final seconds. France’s Maxime-Gaël Ngayap Hambou won bronze with a 10-0 victory over Brazil’s Rafael Macedo, while Greece’s Theodoros Tselidis edged Spain’s Tristani Mosakhlishvili 1-0 for the second bronze. Bekauri continues Georgia’s strong judo legacy, following champions like Shota Chochishvili (1972), Shota Khabareli (1980), David Khakhaleishvili (1992), Zurab Zviadauri (2004), Irakli Tsirekidze (2008), and Lasha Shavdatuashvili (2012). "Gold is my favorite color," Bekauri said. "It was a tough fight. Japanese judoka are incredibly strong, and this match was unbelievable." Murao, after claiming silver, stated simply, "I would like to aim for gold at the Los Angeles Olympics." Ngayap Hambou, competing in his first Olympics at 23, added to his resume after winning mixed team silver at the 2023 and 2024 world championships. "I wanted gold, but I’m still so happy with bronze," he said. Tselidis delivered Greece’s first medal of Paris 2024, defeating close friend Mosakhlishvili. "I made history—for me and for Greece. This feeling is amazing," he said. "He’s my best friend … but it’s sport."

Three-time Olympic wrestling champion Buvaisar Saitiev dies aged 49

Three-time Olympic champion in freestyle wrestling Buvaisar Saitiev has died at the age of 49. This was announced by President of the Russian Wrestling Federation (RWF) Mikhail Mamiashvili, News.Az reports, citing TASS. "They called me just ten minutes ago to inform me of this tragedy. It's hard to believe. We were supposed to fly together tomorrow," Mamiashvili said, deeply shocked by the loss. Saitiev, a three-time Olympic gold medalist in the 74 kg weight category (1996, 2004, and 2008), was also a six-time World and European Championship winner. In recognition of his exceptional career, he was named Honored Master of Sports of Russia in 1995 and was honored with multiple prestigious awards, including the Order of Merit for the Fatherland, IV degree, the Order of Honor, and the Order of Friendship.

Asian Wrestling Championships 2025: Nitesh Wins Bronze in Greco-Roman 97kg

India’s Nitesh secured a bronze medal in the Greco-Roman 97kg category at the Asian Wrestling Championships 2025 in Amman, Jordan, on Wednesday. This marks India’s second medal at the event. The 22-year-old dominated Kazakhstan’s Ilyas Guchigov 9-0 (VSU) in his opening bout but fell to reigning Olympic champion Mohammad Hadi Saravi of Iran by the same score in the semi-finals. In the bronze medal match, Nitesh defeated Turkmenistan’s Amanberdi Agamammedov 9-0 (VSU). Earlier, Sunil Kumar won bronze in the 87kg Greco-Roman category. Saravi clinched gold after defeating Japan’s Yuri Nakazato in the final. Among India’s other Greco-Roman wrestlers, Neeraj (67kg) reached the bronze medal match via repechage but lost 5-0 to Japan’s Katsuaki Endo. Sumit (60kg), Kuldeep Malak (72kg), and Rahul (82kg) failed to reach medal bouts. India’s Paris 2024 Olympians Reetika Hooda (76kg) and Antim Panghal (53kg) will compete on Thursday and Friday.

Geno Petriashvili Wins Gold Medal in Men’s 125kg Freestyle Wrestling at 2024 Paris Olympics

Geno Petriashvili, 30, won his first gold medal at the 2024 Paris Olympics in the men’s 125kg freestyle wrestling event. He survived a late charge from his Iranian opponent Amir Hossein Zare in the final to win 10-9. Geno Petriashvili now holds all Olympic medals, having won his first bronze at the 2016 Rio Olympics and his first silver at the 2021 Tokyo Olympics.
