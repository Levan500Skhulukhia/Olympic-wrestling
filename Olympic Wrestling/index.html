Welcome to Olympic Wrestling

Discover the three Olympic wrestling styles: Judo, Greco-Roman Wrestling, and Freestyle Wrestling. Each has its unique rules, techniques, and legendary athletes.

Judo

Judo, founded in Japan by Jigoro Kano in 1882, is a martial art focused on throws, holds, and submissions. It emphasizes technique and leverage over brute strength.

Greco-Roman Wrestling

Greco-Roman wrestling, an ancient combat sport, allows only upper-body techniques. It has been part of the Olympics since 1896.

Learn More

Freestyle Wrestling

Freestyle wrestling is a dynamic sport where athletes can use both upper and lower body techniques. It demands agility, strength, and strategy.
